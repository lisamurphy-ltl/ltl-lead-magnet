Questions, answered

How much time do business owners waste on repetitive admin?

Owners routinely lose a full day or more each week to repetitive admin — email, meetings, follow-ups, reporting and scheduling. By some estimates it reaches close to a third of the working week. For an owner whose time is worth $150 an hour, that can exceed $100,000 a year in lost capacity.

How do I calculate what my time is worth?

Take your target annual income and divide by the hours you actually work in a year. Most service-business owners value their time between $75 and $400 an hour — which is exactly why doing $30-an-hour admin is so expensive. This calculator does the math for you in about two minutes.

What repetitive tasks can AI take off my plate?

Inbox triage, meeting notes and follow-ups, client and sales follow-ups, referrals, lead nurture, testimonials, relationship management, weekly planning, re-planning under pressure, and weekly review — the ten tasks this tool scores.

How do I delegate to AI without micromanaging?

Hand AI the routine, rules-based work — drafting, summarizing, sorting, scheduling — and keep the judgment calls for yourself. Give it a reusable prompt with your role, voice and context so the output is consistent every time.
